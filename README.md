# X-Ray Diffraction Laboratory Exercise
## TFY4220 Solid State Physics NTNU
Laberatory experiment where X-ray diffractometry was conducted on a known silicon powder sample and an unknown sample. Silicon was confermed to be the known sample, identifying it from the diffractogram using knowledge of its face-centered cubic structure, its structural factor and from Briggs law finding its d-spacing - and comparing to an online scientific database. Similar methods and some extra deductions led us to conclude that the unknown sample was a combination of KCl and NaCl. The experiment was thoroughly descibed in the accompanying scientific report.
