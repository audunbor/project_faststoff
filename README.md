# X-Ray Diffraction Laboratory Exercise
## TFY4220 Solid State Physics NTNU
Laberatory experiment where X-ray diffractometry was conducted on a known silicon powder sample and an unknown sample. Using knowledge of the face-centered cubic structure of Silicon and its structural factor we  could deduce its d-spacings from Briggs law, and use these to estimate the lattice constant of Silicon. Comparing the diffractogram peaks and intensities to an online scientific database, Silicon was confirmed to be the known sample. Similar methods and some extra deductions led us to conclude that the unknown sample was a combination of KCl and NaCl. The experiment was descibed in the accompanying scientific report.
