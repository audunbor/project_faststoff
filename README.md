# X-Ray Diffraction Laboratory Exercise
## TFY4220 Solid State Physics NTNU
Laberatory experiment where X-ray diffractometry was conducted on a known silicon powder sample and an unknown sample. Using knowledge of the face-centered cubic structure of Silicon, its structural factor and finding its d-spacing from Briggs law- and comparing to an online scientific database, Silicon was confirmed to be the known sample. Similar methods and some extra deductions led us to conclude that the unknown sample was a combination of KCl and NaCl. The experiment was thoroughly descibed in the accompanying scientific report.
